# Executor Edunelian

*Black Dossier – Kingdom of Ankou*

---

## Basic Information

**Full Name:** Edunelian (no recorded surname)
**Title:** Executor of Ankou
**Age:** Appears 24–26
**Actual Age:** Unknown (records suggest 27 winters since his discovery)
**Height:** 6'4" (193 cm)
**Weight (in armor):** ~84 kg
**Weight (unarmored):** ~75 kg
**Rank:** Highest Judicial Knight of Ankou
**Allegiance:** The Oath of Ankou

---

## Physical Appearance (Unarmored)

* Very tall, long-limbed, imposing even without armor
* Lean but powerful build — more predator than brute
* Skin extremely pale, almost ash-toned
* Hair: Black, straight, shoulder-length when unbound
* Eyes: Naturally dark, but glow crimson when invoking the Oath
* Expression: Calm, unreadable, unnervingly still
* Scars: Surprisingly few — one thin scar across ribs (unknown origin)

He moves quietly. Even barefoot, he makes little sound.

---

## Presence & Aura

* Voice is low and layered — sometimes sounds like two tones overlapping
* Body temperature runs colder than average
* Rarely blinks when focused
* Makes prolonged eye contact, which unsettles most people
* Has a habit of tilting his head slightly when assessing someone

Animals react strangely:

* Horses grow tense
* Crows gather
* Dogs either whimper or sit calmly

---

## Armor & Gear Details

**Armor:**

* Forged of abyssal iron blessed in the Catacombs
* Jagged, angular plating designed to intimidate
* Helmet shaped like a predatory beak
* Crimson spectral ribbon trails from helm (manifestation of Oath energy)
* Tattered cloak fragments resemble funeral veils

**Weapon:**

* Execution Spear (12 ft total length)
* Crimson blade never rusts, never dulls
* Chains wrapped along the haft tighten when a decree is legitimate
* Slight blood-like residue permanently stains the edge

---

## Combat Profile

* Fighting Style: Precise, economical, no wasted motion
* Specializes in thrust-based lethal strikes
* Extremely high pain tolerance
* Can fight in near-total darkness
* Rarely speaks mid-combat

He does not rage.
He does not taunt.
He ends.

---

## Habits & Small Details

* Drinks water, not wine
* Rarely eats meat
* Sleeps upright more often than lying down
* Keeps no personal belongings
* Removes armor only in solitude

He does not celebrate victories.

He records names.

---

## Psychological Profile

* Not cruel, not merciful — strictly balanced
* Dislikes corruption more than rebellion
* Values order over comfort
* Struggles internally with the difference between obedience and justice

His greatest fear?

That one day the Oath will demand **his own name.**

## VIII. The Marsh of Unburied Kings

Before Edunelian ever turned his spear upon a noble of Ankou, he was sent beyond its borders—into the Marsh of Unburied Kings.

The marsh was a graveyard that refused to rot. Armor rose from the mire like rusted ribs. Crowns lay half-sunk in black water. Those who died there did not decay; they lingered, bloated with unfinished will.

Ankou’s scouts had vanished. Entire patrols swallowed whole.

Edunelian walked into the fog alone.

The marsh tested him with voices. It whispered his name in tones that resembled childhood laughter. It showed him shapes in the water—two figures reaching for him, faceless yet familiar. Promises of warmth. Promises of belonging.

He did not answer.

When the first corpse-king rose, stitched together by vines and spite, Edunelian tilted his head. Listening.

The chains on his spear tightened.

What followed was not battle. It was correction.

He moved through the marsh like a verdict delivered in slow, inevitable strokes. Each thrust of the crimson blade severed the will binding corpse to crown. Each fallen king sank quietly, as if finally permitted rest.

When he returned to Ankou, the fog did not cling to him.

It recoiled.

## IX. The Girl Who Did Not Die

Not all decrees end in blood.

One winter, a seal arrived bearing the name of a fourteen-year-old girl accused of witchcraft. The charge was thin. The villagers were frightened. The local lord desired spectacle.

Edunelian arrived at dusk.

The girl did not beg. She stared at him with steady eyes. “If I am guilty,” she said, “then the mist will swallow me.”

The decree was sealed. The Oath was clear.

Edunelian extended his clawed gauntlet—not toward her throat, but toward the villagers. The chains of his spear began to rattle, louder than they ever had.

The lord stepped forward, indignant.

The chains snapped taut.

Edunelian’s visor glowed brighter than the setting sun.

“I serve the Oath,” he said.

The wax seal on the decree cracked—black turned brittle, then ash.

The true accusation surfaced: the lord’s cruelty, his theft of harvest, his murders disguised as superstition.

By nightfall, the village had a new grave.

The girl lived.

From that day, rumors changed. The Executor was no longer only feared.

He was watched.

## X. Beneath the Armor

There are few truths about Edunelian.

He does sleep, though no one knows when. He eats rarely. He bleeds—but the blood is darker than most, thick as ink.

Beneath the helm, his face bears no scars. No sigils. Only pallor, as if light has not touched him in years. His eyes, when unmasked in the chapel’s solitude, burn with a steady crimson—not rage, not hunger, but awareness.

He speaks sometimes to the altar.

Not prayers.

Questions.

“Is justice the same as obedience?”

The cathedral never answers.

Yet the bells ring.

## XI. The Kingdom Cracks

Power festers in closed rooms.

A faction within Ankou’s council grew restless with the Executor’s independence. They desired control over him—over what he symbolized. They began forging decrees. False seals. Names of political rivals marked for death.

The first forged decree reached him under moonlight.

He held it between gauntleted fingers. The wax shimmered wrong. The ink trembled.

The chains did not move.

Edunelian walked not to the named target—but to the council chamber.

Guards barred his path.

He did not draw his spear.

He simply stepped forward.

Armor groaned. Marble split. The guards stepped aside.

Inside, the councilors trembled behind polished masks of authority.

“You were not named,” one whispered.

Edunelian placed the forged decree upon the table.

The chains coiled slowly around the parchment.

“You forgot,” he said, voice layered and low, “that I listen.”

By dawn, three seats in the council were empty.

The High Cleric did not interfere.

## XII. The Sovereign’s Shadow

It is said now that Edunelian is not merely an Executor—but the Kingdom’s conscience given form.

Some nights, the mist gathers around him as though kneeling. Crows follow him not as scavengers, but as witnesses. The crimson ribbon trailing from his helm seems longer, darker—like a tether stretching somewhere unseen.

There are moments—rare and fleeting—when he pauses mid-strike, as if hearing something new. A whisper deeper than the Oath. Older than Ankou.

Some believe the Sovereign of Death does not command him.

Some believe it waits for him.

And in the oldest prophecy, hidden beneath layers of forbidden scripture, there is a final line scratched in trembling ink:

*When the Executor judges the throne, the Kingdom will either be reborn… or buried without a bell.*

## XIII. The Weight of a Name

Edunelian stands now at the cathedral’s highest spire, overlooking a kingdom balanced on fracture.

Below him, nobles feast.
In the marsh, something stirs again.
In the villages, hope grows in fragile whispers.

He does not smile.
He does not mourn.

He waits.

Because somewhere, in ink not yet dry, a name is being written.

And when the wax seal hardens—
when the chains tighten—
when the mist parts—

The Kingdom of Ankou will learn whether its Executor is shield…

or scythe.
